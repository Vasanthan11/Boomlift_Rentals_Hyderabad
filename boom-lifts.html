Height Bands for Boom Lift Rentals

We classify booms into simple height bands so your team can quickly understand which range to ask for when planning workfronts.

Compact booms
30–45 ft working height

Best for indoor atriums, low-rise façade, mezzanine plant work and installations below 4–5 floors where access is tight.

Mid-range booms
60–80 ft working height

The most requested category for large warehouses, plant structures, malls and IT park façades up to ~8–10 floors.

High reach booms
100–150 ft working height

Used for chimneys, tall industrial buildings, stadium roofs and long-span infrastructure where scaffolding is impractical.

Short FAQs

  • Do you provide operators with the boom lift?

    Yes, most clients prefer our trained operators. If you have in-house operators, we can also supply the machine on “bare rental”.

  • What information do you need for a quote?

    Site location, approximate height, job duration, floor condition, access constraints and whether work is indoor or outdoor.

  • How early should we book a boom?

    For peak season projects, 2–3 days notice is ideal. Same-day dispatch may be possible inside city limits if a suitable boom is free.

  • Can the same boom be shifted within our campus?

    Yes. Our team can guide your engineers on safe repositioning between workfronts or we can send support staff to assist.
